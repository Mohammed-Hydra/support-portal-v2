# Client Support Plan (Kuwait, KWD)

This document is a ready-to-use blueprint for acquiring small-company clients in Kuwait and delivering profitable technical support using the **HYDRA-TECH Support Portal v2** (portal + email + WhatsApp), with clear packages, SLAs, onboarding, and pricing.

---

## 1) Target customers (who we serve)

### Ideal client profile
- **Company size**: 51–100 users (primary). We will also offer tiers for 1–50 and 101–200 later.
- **Needs**: Microsoft 365 administration, endpoint support, Wi‑Fi/network troubleshooting, backups/restore assistance, and “one place” to track support.
- **Buyer**: Office manager, operations manager, finance manager, or owner (often not a technical buyer).

### Positioning statement
> We provide a managed helpdesk with clear SLAs, hybrid support (remote + onsite), and full visibility through a portal—so your team gets fast help and management gets control.

---

## 2) Service model (how support is delivered)

### Channels
- **Portal**: primary channel (tickets, SLA, updates, reporting).
- **Email ingestion** (optional): for clients who prefer email.
- **WhatsApp ingestion** (optional): to capture urgent requests, but still convert to tickets for visibility.

### Hybrid delivery
- Remote-first resolution where possible (faster, cheaper).
- Onsite for network/hardware or when remote is not efficient.

### Scope included (baseline)
- Helpdesk: ticketing, triage, reporting.
- Endpoints: Windows/Mac troubleshooting, user profile issues, printer setup help, basic patch guidance.
- Microsoft 365: user management, mail/Teams basics, licensing changes, mail flow troubleshooting (where permitted).
- Network basics: Wi‑Fi/router/firewall basic troubleshooting and vendor coordination.
- Backup assistance: restore help and basic monitoring guidance (not full SOC/EDR unless added).

### Out of scope (billed separately)
- Major projects (migrations, new network design, large rollouts).
- Procurement and hardware replacement (can be provided as separate service).
- Custom software development.
- Full 24x7 SOC monitoring unless explicitly included.

## 3) Packages & inclusions

We offer 3 clear packages with simple upsell paths.

### Package A — Essential (Remote-first)
- **Coverage**: 8x5 business hours
- **Remote support**: included
- **Onsite**: not included (billable if required)
- **Best for**: stable environments that mostly need remote help + M365 admin

### Package B — Standard (Hybrid)
Everything in Essential, plus:
- **Onsite included**: 4 hours / month (scheduled)
- **Preventive**: monthly health check (M365 + endpoints basics)
- **Best for**: most SMBs who need occasional onsite support

### Package C — Premium (Hybrid + priority)
Everything in Standard, plus:
- **Onsite included**: 8 hours / month (scheduled or priority onsite)
- **Priority queue** + faster SLA targets
- **Best for**: time-sensitive operations (e.g., retail, healthcare clinics, logistics)

### Add-ons (optional)
- **After-hours emergency coverage** (on-call)
- **Backup monitoring** (formal monitoring tool + monthly restore test)
- **Security add-on** (EDR/monitoring) for Premium only
- **User onboarding/offboarding automation** (process + templates)

## 4) SLA policy (what we guarantee)

### Priority definitions
- **Critical**: business is down, many users blocked, security incident
- **High**: major impact, multiple users affected, work significantly impaired
- **Medium**: normal request, workaround exists
- **Low**: minor issue, how-to, cosmetic, non-urgent

### Response SLAs (targets)
These are reasonable starting targets for Kuwait SMBs; adjust after 2–3 clients based on actual volume.

| Priority | Essential (8x5) | Standard (8x5) | Premium (8x5 + priority) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Critical | ≤ 60 min | ≤ 30 min | ≤ 15 min |
| High | ≤ 4 hours | ≤ 2 hours | ≤ 1 hour |
| Medium | ≤ 8 hours | ≤ 6 hours | ≤ 4 hours |
| Low | ≤ 2 business days | ≤ 1 business day | ≤ 1 business day |

### “Waiting User” workflow
- If we need input, ticket status becomes **Waiting User**.
- We notify the requester with clear instructions (what to send, screenshots, access details).
- After **X days** no response (recommended: 5 business days), ticket may be auto-closed (configurable by client).

### Escalation
Escalate when:
- SLA at risk (approaching breach)
- repeated issue / systemic issue detected
- external vendor involvement required

## 5) Pricing (KWD) — recommended model

### Recommended pricing model
Use **Base fee + per-user/month**. This keeps pricing simple, scales with client size, and protects you from high-ticket-volume clients.

**Why not fixed-only pricing?** Ticket volume varies widely between “50 users” companies; base + per-user reduces the risk of underpricing.

### Package pricing (starting point)
These are baseline numbers to start selling. Adjust after 2–3 clients based on real ticket volumes and onsite usage.

| Package | Monthly base (KWD) | Per user/month (KWD) | Onsite included | Best for |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Essential | 250 | 6 | Not included | Remote-first support |
| Standard | 350 | 8 | 4 hours/month | Most SMBs |
| Premium | 450 | 10 | 8 hours/month | Priority + more onsite |

### Example invoices (51–100 users)

| Users | Essential | Standard | Premium |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 60 users | 250 + (60×6)=610 | 350 + (60×8)=830 | 450 + (60×10)=1,050 |
| 80 users | 250 + (80×6)=730 | 350 + (80×8)=990 | 450 + (80×10)=1,250 |
| 100 users | 250 + (100×6)=850 | 350 + (100×8)=1,150 | 450 + (100×10)=1,450 |

### Add-ons & overage pricing
- **Additional onsite hours** (beyond included): **KWD 25–35/hour** (define travel radius; outside radius billed separately)
- **After-hours emergency coverage**: + **15–30%** of monthly fee *or* per-incident retainer (define what qualifies as “emergency”)
- **Projects** (migrations/rollouts): fixed quote or time & materials (recommended: **KWD 25–45/hour** depending on seniority)
- **Backup monitoring** (tooling + monthly restore test): priced as add-on per client (or per server/workload)

### Fair-use policy (prevents abuse)
Define fair-use caps per package (example):
- Essential: **up to 6 hours remote/month included** (soft cap; enforce only for chronic abuse)
- Standard: **up to 10 hours remote + 4 hours onsite/month**
- Premium: **up to 16 hours remote + 8 hours onsite/month**

If usage exceeds fair-use for 2 consecutive months, move the client to a higher tier or bill overage at agreed hourly rate.

## 6) Client acquisition (how we get clients)

### Offer that converts
Use a simple, low-friction entry offer:
- **Free 30-minute assessment + portal demo**
- A short deliverable: “Top 5 issues + quick wins”
- Incentive: **discount on onboarding** if they sign within 7 days

### Lead channels (run in parallel)
- **Referrals / partners**
  - Network equipment vendors, CCTV vendors, ISP resellers, accounting firms, web agencies
  - Give a referral commission or fixed referral fee
- **Outbound**
  - Target: operations managers / office managers at SMEs
  - Sequence: WhatsApp → call → email follow-up → meeting
- **Inbound**
  - 1-page landing page with “Packages + SLAs + pricing range” and a meeting link
  - SEO keywords: “IT support Kuwait”, “Microsoft 365 support Kuwait”, “helpdesk Kuwait”
- **Networking**
  - Local business groups + LinkedIn outreach

### Outreach scripts (copy/paste)

**WhatsApp (first message):**
> Hi {Name}, I’m {YourName} from HYDRA-TECH. We provide managed IT support for SMEs in Kuwait (portal + WhatsApp + onsite). If I can help you reduce IT issues and response time, can we schedule a 15-minute call this week?

**Email (follow-up):**
Subject: Managed IT support with SLA (Kuwait)
> Hi {Name},\n>\n> We help SMEs manage day-to-day IT (M365, endpoints, Wi‑Fi/network) with clear SLAs and full visibility through a portal.\n>\n> Would you like a free 30-minute assessment? I’ll share a quick summary of top issues + improvements.\n>\n> Regards,\n> {Signature}

**Discovery call questions (10–15 minutes):**
- How many users/devices?
- Microsoft 365 / email setup? Any recurring issues?
- Biggest pain point today (response time, visibility, security, backups)?
- Any critical systems (POS, clinics, logistics apps)?
- Do you need onsite support monthly?

## 7) Client onboarding (how we start service)

### Onboarding timeline (recommended)
- **Day 0–2**: access + discovery
- **Day 3–5**: setup + portal rollout
- **Day 6–10**: stabilization + first report

### Onboarding checklist
**Business**
- Signed agreement + SLA + pricing schedule
- Primary contacts (owner, office manager, finance)
- Approved communication channels (portal/email/WhatsApp)

**Technical access**
- Microsoft 365 admin access (least privilege where possible)
- Device inventory (users, laptops, desktops, printers)
- Network overview (ISP, router/firewall model, Wi‑Fi setup)
- Backup overview (what is backed up, where, last restore test)

**Portal rollout**
- Create internal users (admin/agents) and client requesters
- Configure requester portal and share the requester link
- Define priority definitions and what counts as “Critical”
- Agree on ticket categories (software/hardware/network/access/other)

**Operational**
- Escalation contacts and approvals (e.g., license purchase approval)
- Maintenance windows and onsite scheduling rules

## 8) Operating model (how we deliver support)

### Triage rules
- First response: acknowledge + gather missing info quickly.
- Use **Waiting User** when blocked by client input.
- Convert WhatsApp/email into a ticket so the client can track progress.

### Weekly routine (internal)
- Review: open tickets, SLA at risk, repeated issues.
- Identify 1–2 “root cause” fixes (e.g., Wi‑Fi AP placement, mailbox quota, device patching).

### Monthly client report (send to management)
Include:
- Ticket volume and top categories
- Response time and resolution time
- SLA breaches and reasons
- Workload summary and improvements done
- Recommendations for next month

## 9) KPIs (how we measure success)

### Operational KPIs
- **First response time** (avg + by priority)
- **Resolution time** (avg + by category)
- **SLA breaches** (count + reasons)
- **Reopen rate** (signals quality)
- **Backlog** (open tickets by status)

### Client experience KPIs (optional)
- CSAT after ticket closure (simple 1–5 rating)
- Net promoter (quarterly, optional)

## 10) Contract basics (reduce risk)

### Recommended commercial terms
- Minimum term: **6–12 months** (recommended 12 with discounted onboarding)
- Billing: monthly in advance
- Payment terms: 7–14 days
- Included hours and overage rates clearly stated

### Client responsibilities
- Provide timely access and approvals
- Maintain licensed software where required
- Use supported hardware and basic security hygiene (MFA, password policy)

### Exclusions
- Projects and new deployments not listed in package
- Third-party vendor downtime beyond your control (document coordination vs responsibility)
